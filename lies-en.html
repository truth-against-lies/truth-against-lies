"Israel committed genocide in Gaza"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

Israel's military operations in Gaza following October 7, 2023, constitute genocide — the deliberate and systematic destruction of the Palestinian people. South Africa filed a case at the International Court of Justice (ICJ). The claim is widespread on social media and appears in international public discourse.

Factual Analysis:
  • Legal definition: Genocide requires specific intent (dolus specialis) to destroy a national, ethnic, or religious group. The UN Convention on the Prevention of Genocide (1948, Article II) stipulates that intent must be proven, not merely the existence of casualties.
  • The International Court of Justice (ICJ) did not rule that genocide occurred. In its interim ruling of January 2024, it determined that South Africa's case was "plausible enough to be heard" — the lowest legal threshold for interim proceedings, not a judgment.
  • Combatant-to-civilian ratio: Prof. John Spencer, head of urban warfare research at West Point, determined that Israel's casualty ratio was approximately 1:1.5, while the global average in urban warfare is estimated, per a contested assessment, at approximately 1:9.
  • Documented warning measures: The IDF made 50,066 personal phone calls, sent 12 million voice messages and 14 million SMS, dropped millions of leaflets, and created evacuation corridors. According to available data, there is no precedent for warning measures of this scope in other armed conflicts.
  • Use of human shields: UNRWA (UN agency) reported finding rockets in its schools on 3 separate occasions. NATO published a documented report on Hamas's human shield strategy. Yahya Sinwar was quoted as saying: "We decided to turn the bodies of our women and children into a dam."
  • General Sir John McColl (Chair of the HLMG, former UK Chief of the Defence Staff) stated: «No military in history has taken such comprehensive steps to warn a civilian population.» The HLMG — a group of 20+ former generals and defense ministers from NATO countries — submitted an amicus brief to the ICC stating that IDF operations complied with the laws of war.
  • ICJ ruling, January 2024: The International Court of Justice did not determine that genocide was occurring. Judge Joan Donoghue (ICJ President) clarified: «The Court has not at this stage decided whether South Africa is correct in its claims.»

It should be noted that a military with advanced air capabilities that systematically employs comprehensive warning measures is inconsistent with an intent to destroy. According to the academic literature, there is no historical precedent for a genocide in which the attacking side warns the population in advance.

Sources

  2. International Court of Justice (ICJ), Interim Order — South Africa v. Israel (January 26, 2024)
  3. Atlantic Council, Expert Analysis — The Gaza Conflict (2024)
  4. John Spencer, Israel Has Created a New Standard for Urban Warfare, Newsweek (2024)

"Israel pushed Trump to fight Iran"

★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Claim:

Israel controlled, manipulated, or "pushed" the United States — particularly President Trump — into a confrontation with Iran. According to this narrative, the US is fighting "Israel's war" and is not defending its own interests. This claim is identified by the ADL as a modern version of the "dual loyalty" trope — a historically documented antisemitic pattern.

Factual Analysis:
  • The US-Iran confrontation predates any Israeli connection: The 1953 CIA coup (Mossadegh), the 1979 hostage crisis (444 days), and the complete diplomatic break since 1980 — decades before claims of Israeli influence.
  • American casualties from Iranian activity: According to FDD data, Iran is responsible for the deaths of 869+ Americans: the 1983 Beirut bombing (241 soldiers), the 1996 Khobar Towers bombing (19), and IEDs in Iraq 2003–2011 (603+). A US court ordered Iran to pay $1.5 billion in damages.
  • Early American stance: In a 1980 NBC interview with Rona Barrett, Trump expressed an anti-Iran position and argued that the US should have invaded Iran. This stance preceded claims of Israeli influence by 46 years.
  • Independent American interests: The Strait of Hormuz (~20% of global oil), Iran's nuclear program, and terror financing (Hezbollah, Hamas, Houthis). Iran has been designated a "state sponsor of terrorism" by the US State Department since 1984.
  • Multi-state pressure: The Washington Post reported that the pressure came from "an unusual pairing of allies: Israel and Saudi Arabia." Notably, Saudi pressure is not characterized as a "conspiracy" in public discourse.
  • The JCPOA (2015): It contained sunset clauses — restrictions expiring in 2025–2030, allowing Iran a legal path to nuclear capability. Opposition to the deal was not exclusively Israeli — Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Egypt opposed it with equal intensity.
  • Regarding the claim of $150 billion: According to US Treasury Department data, the amount unfrozen for Iran was ~$50 billion (frozen Iranian assets), not $150B. Of that, only ~$3 billion was liquid — the rest consisted of debts and contracts.
  • According to the IAEA report (2024): After the US withdrawal from the deal (2018), Iran enriched uranium to 60%+ (weapons-grade threshold: 90%). Its enriched uranium stockpile is 30 times the amount permitted under the deal. This risk exists regardless of Israel.

The ADL classifies this narrative as a version of the "dual loyalty" trope — accusing Jews of secretly directing foreign policy. This trope is documented historically in the Dreyfus Affair (1894), the "stab in the back myth" (1918), and Stalin's "Doctors' Plot" (1953).

"Israel is an apartheid state"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

Israel operates a system of racial segregation similar to the apartheid regime in South Africa. Organizations such as Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch have published reports using this label. "Israeli Apartheid Week" is held on 250+ campuses worldwide.

Factual Analysis:
  • The apartheid regime in South Africa: Black people could not vote, were banned from entering white neighborhoods, separate and inferior education was mandated by law, "Whites Only" signs were placed in public spaces, and interracial marriages constituted a criminal offense.
  • The legal situation in Israel: Arab citizens (21% of the population) have held full and equal voting rights since 1948. Justice Salim Joubran (Arab) served as Deputy President of the Supreme Court. The Ra'am party joined the governing coalition in 2021. Druze and Bedouin serve as senior officers in the IDF.
  • Shared public spaces: Arab and Jewish citizens study at the same universities, are treated at the same hospitals, and use the same public facilities. There is no institutional segregation in public spaces.
  • Kenneth Meshoe, a South African member of parliament who lived under the apartheid regime, stated: "I have visited Israel many times. Nothing there can be compared to apartheid in South Africa."
  • Justice Richard Goldstone (South Africa), who investigated Israel for the UN, wrote in the New York Times (2011): "In Israel, there is no apartheid. Nothing there comes close to the definition."
  • Justice Khaled Kabub (Arab Muslim) serves on the Supreme Court — the highest judicial body in the country. This fact is inconsistent with the definition of apartheid, which by its nature prevents minority groups from serving in governing institutions.
  • According to the Ministry of Health, 25% of Israel's physicians are Arab. Justice George Karra (Arab Christian from Jaffa) served on the Supreme Court. Lubna Azzam was appointed as the first Arab police commander (2023). The 25th Knesset includes 10 Arab members of Knesset from 4 parties.

"Jews control the media and the global economy"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

Jews secretly control banks, media, and world governments, manipulating global events to their benefit. This theory is based in part on the "Protocols of the Elders of Zion" — a document proven to be a forgery (created around 1903) — and on narratives concerning the Rothschild family.

Factual Analysis:
  • "Protocols of the Elders of Zion" — a documented forgery: The document was created by the Russian secret police (Okhrana). In 1921, the Times of London proved it was a direct copy of a French satire about Napoleon III (1864) that had nothing to do with Jews. A Swiss court (1935) declared the text a "forgery, plagiarism, and obscene literature" (overturned on appeal in 1937 on procedural grounds, but the findings of forgery were not refuted).
  • Rothschild family data: The family's wealth is estimated at $400 billion, spread across 7 generations. For comparison: Elon Musk alone is worth ~$800 billion. The claim that "the Rothschilds control 80% of the world's wealth" fails basic arithmetic — total global wealth is estimated at ~$471 trillion.
  • According to S&P Global data, the 4 largest banks in the world are Chinese (ICBC, Agricultural Bank, Construction Bank, Bank of China). Of the top 20 banks, most are Chinese, Japanese, British, and French.
  • US media ownership: 6 companies control 90% of US media (Comcast, Disney, Warner Bros. Discovery, Paramount, Fox, Sony). Comcast (Roberts family — of Russian-Jewish descent), Disney (led by Bob Iger — a salaried executive, not an owner), Fox (Rupert Murdoch — Australian), Sony (Japanese). AP is a nonprofit. It should be noted that the ethnic background of a business owner does not constitute evidence of "group control" but reflects individual business success.
  • Demographic data: Jews comprise 0.2% of the world's population (15.8 million out of 8 billion). Statistically, a claim of global control by a group of this size is implausible.
  • As the ADL notes: "Throughout history, Jews have had little control even over their own fate" — referring to expulsions, pogroms, and the Holocaust.

"Jews have no historical connection to the Land of Israel"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

According to this claim, Jews are "European colonizers" with no historical connection to the Land of Israel, and Zionism is merely a modern "colonial project." Mahmoud Abbas (April 2025) claimed the Temples stood in Yemen, not Jerusalem. The "Temple Denial" movement denies any Jewish connection to the Temple Mount.

Factual Analysis — 3,200 Years of Evidence:
  • Merneptah Stele (1208 BCE): An Egyptian inscription constituting the earliest non-biblical mention of Israel in history. Displayed in the Cairo Museum.
  • Tel Dan Stele (9th century BCE): An Aramaic inscription discovered in 1993, mentioning "House of David" — the first external evidence of the Davidic dynasty.
  • Siloam Inscription (701 BCE): An inscription in ancient Hebrew inside Hezekiah's Tunnel in Jerusalem, attesting to the presence of Hebrew speakers in Jerusalem 2,700 years ago.
  • Dead Sea Scrolls (3rd century BCE — 1st century CE): 981 manuscripts in Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek — the oldest copies of the Bible. Displayed at the Israel Museum, Jerusalem.
  • Arch of Titus, Rome (81 CE): A relief depicting Roman soldiers carrying the menorah from the Temple in Jerusalem — Roman evidence of the existence of the Jewish Temple in Jerusalem.
  • Bar Kokhba coins (132–135 CE): 50,000+ coins with Jewish symbols and inscriptions in ancient Hebrew: "For the freedom of Jerusalem."
  • Genetic studies (PNAS 2000, Nature 2010): All Jewish groups — Ashkenazi, Sephardi, and Mizrahi — share common Middle Eastern genetic markers. The studies indicate that Jews are genetically closer to Palestinians, Syrians, and Druze than to Europeans.
  • League of Nations (1922): Officially recognized "the historical connection of the Jewish people with Palestine."
  • Archaeological findings 2024–2025: The Siloam Inscription (2024, new decipherment) — 6 lines in ancient Hebrew describing the digging of Hezekiah's Tunnel. David's Palace from Khirbet Qeiyafa (3,000 years old). LMLK stamps (500+) from the Kingdom of Judah. A 2023 DNA survey (Bronze Age Levant) found that 50%+ of the genome of modern Jews originates from the ancient population of the Levant.

The totality of evidence — archaeology, Roman records, genetic science, and international law — points to a continuous historical connection of the Jewish people to the Land of Israel spanning more than 3,200 years.

"Israel created Hamas"

★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Claim:

According to this claim, Israel deliberately created, funded, and maintained Hamas to divide the Palestinians and weaken the PLO. From this, it is inferred that Israel bears responsibility for Hamas's actions, including the October 7 attack.

Factual Analysis:
  • Historical context: In the 1970s and 1980s, Israel permitted the activities of Sheikh Ahmed Yassin and the "Mujama al-Islamiyya" movement (a religious-social organization) — as a counterweight to Arafat's PLO, which was carrying out attacks. This was a social organization, not a military one.
  • Hamas was founded in 1987 as a branch of the Muslim Brotherhood (an Egyptian organization established in 1928). Ideologically, Hamas is a product of the Muslim Brotherhood movement, not an Israeli creation.
  • Israel cut all ties with the body once its nature became clear. Yassin was arrested in 1989 and imprisoned for 8 years. He was eliminated in 2004 in an Israeli strike.
  • Historical parallel: The US armed the mujahideen in Afghanistan against the USSR — some of whom later became al-Qaeda. This dynamic, in which a supported entity turns hostile, is documented in numerous international relations cases.
  • Actual Hamas funding: According to US Treasury Department data and Washington Post reports: Iran ($100M–$350M/year), Qatar ($1.8 billion, 2012–2023). Israel does not appear as a funder.

The claim that "Israel created Hamas" oversimplifies a complex historical reality. The evidence indicates that Hamas was founded as a branch of an existing ideological movement and was funded primarily by Iran and Qatar.

"From the River to the Sea — it's just a call for freedom"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

"From the river to the sea, Palestine will be free" is an innocuous slogan for liberation and Palestinian rights. It contains no call for violence or the destruction of Israel.

Factual Analysis:
  • 2017 Hamas Charter, Article 2: Defines "Palestine" as «from the Jordan River to the Mediterranean Sea» — an area encompassing all of the State of Israel. Article 20: «Hamas does not recognize any alternative solution to the liberation of all of Palestine.»
  • Geographic meaning: Between the Jordan and the Sea live 7.2 million Jews. Implementing the slogan as defined in the Hamas Charter means the elimination of the State of Israel.
  • The ADL classifies the slogan as «an antisemitic slogan calling for the dismantling of the Jewish state of Israel.» In April 2024, the US Congress condemned the use of the slogan by a vote of 377–44 (H.Res.883).
  • Historical source: The slogan appeared as early as 1964 in the PLO Charter (PLO Charter), Article 2: "Palestine within the boundaries of the Mandate period is an indivisible territorial unit" — a definition encompassing all of Israel's territory.
  • Parallel: For comparison, a similar slogan "Germany from the Rhine to the Oder will be free" would be interpreted as a call to abolish the sovereignty of Poland and the Czech Republic, not as a "call for liberation."

"Israel is conducting ethnic cleansing"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

Israel is conducting systematic ethnic cleansing of the Palestinians — expelling them from their lands, destroying homes and villages, and attempting to eliminate the Palestinian presence.

Factual Analysis:
  • Demographic data: According to PCBS and World Bank data, the Palestinian population grew from 1.3 million (1990) to 5.5 million (2024) in the West Bank and Gaza — a growth rate of 323%.
  • Gaza: From 700,000 (1990) to ~2.1 million (2023) — a growth of ~200%. This figure is inconsistent with the definition of ethnic cleansing.
  • Arab citizens of Israel: Grew from 156,000 (1948) to 2.1 million (2024) — a 13-fold increase. By comparison, in every documented case of ethnic cleansing, the targeted population declined.
  • Comparison with known cases: Srebrenica (1995) — 8,000 killed in 10 days. Rwanda (1994) — 800,000 in 100 days. Darfur — 300,000+ killed, millions displaced. In each case, a sharp demographic decline was recorded.
  • Conceptual distinction: It is important to distinguish between a military conflict with civilian casualties (a phenomenon characterizing all urban warfare) and a deliberate policy of population removal. The demographic data do not support the latter characterization.

In summary, population growth of 323% during the period in question is inconsistent with the definition of ethnic cleansing as accepted in international law and academic literature.

"Israel steals organs from Palestinians"

★★★★★Evidence Strength
Claim:

Israel kidnaps and murders Palestinians for the purpose of harvesting their organs. The claim is based on an article published in the Swedish newspaper Aftonbladet (2009) and received widespread dissemination on social media and in Arab media.

Factual Analysis:
  • The source: A single article by Donald Bostrom in Aftonbladet (August 2009). Bostrom himself admitted he had no direct evidence — the article was based on "hearsay testimony" and "suspicions."
  • Swedish government response: Swedish Foreign Minister Carl Bildt refused to condemn the article, citing press freedom. It should be noted that Bildt did not confirm the article's content. This case illustrates how claims lacking a factual basis can receive protection under the principle of freedom of expression.
  • No evidence was found in any international investigation: neither the UN, human rights organizations, nor any independent medical body confirmed the claims.
  • ADL classification: The ADL identified the article as a «modern blood libel» — a continuation of a medieval antisemitic trope accusing Jews of using Christian blood, with "organs" replacing "blood" in the modern version.
  • Relevant fact: Israel is a global leader in organ donation. The Organ Transplant Law (2008) created an advanced donation system. In 2024, Israel donated organs to Palestinians from Gaza who were treated in Israeli hospitals.

"Israel deliberately targets journalists"

★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Claim:

Israel deliberately targets Palestinian journalists to silence reporting on its operations. CPJ (Committee to Protect Journalists) reports dozens of journalists killed in Gaza.

Factual Analysis:
  • CPJ methodology: CPJ counts every "journalist" killed in the combat zone, including cases in which those counted were identified Hamas operatives who held press credentials.
  • Issuance of press credentials: Israel Hayom and HonestReporting documented cases in which Hamas fighters carried press credentials. Hamas confirmed this practice in various contexts.
  • Al Jazeera case: In 2023, Al Jazeera fired an employee who posted content on social media celebrating the October 7 attack. This case illustrates the difficulty of distinguishing between journalism and activism in the conflict zone.
  • Investigation mechanisms: The IDF operates an investigation system for every incident in which a civilian is killed, including journalists. Several cases led to changes in procedures.
  • Comparative data: According to CPJ data: in Syria (2011–2023) 700+ journalists were killed, in Mexico 160+ since 2000, in Ukraine 15+ since 2022. These conflicts did not receive the same level of attribution of "deliberate targeting" in international discourse.

The killing of journalists in combat zones is a documented phenomenon in armed conflicts worldwide. In the absence of evidence for deliberate targeting as policy, there is insufficient factual basis to draw such a conclusion.
