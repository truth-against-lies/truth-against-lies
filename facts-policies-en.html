📜 Policy Decisions — Security Impact Analysis

Review of policy decisions that affected Israel's defense capabilities

The Oslo Accords

1993 — Yitzhak Rabin and Shimon Peres
Under the Oslo II Accords, Israel transferred to the Palestinian Authority 15,000 automatic rifles, 240 heavy machine guns, and 45 armored vehicles. The PA expanded its weapons stockpile beyond the authorized amount. Some of these weapons were used against Israelis during the Second Intifada.
⚠ The outcome:

The Second Intifada (2000–2005): 1,137 Israeli deaths. Some were shot with weapons Israel itself had provided.

Sources: Oslo Accords | Commission of Inquiry | IDF Archives | Ynet

The Jibril Deal

1985 — Government of Shimon Peres
Israel released 1,150 security prisoners (including murderers) in exchange for 3 Israeli soldiers. Among those released: Ahmed Yassin — who went on to found and strengthen Hamas.
⚠ The outcome:

Hamas became the largest armed organization in Gaza. The deal is regarded by researchers as one of the most significant strategic errors in Israel's history.

Sources: IDF Archives | Ynet | Wikipedia (Jibril Deal)

The Tennenbaum Deal

2004 — Government of Ariel Sharon
Israel released 400+ prisoners (including murderers) and terrorist remains in exchange for Elhanan Tennenbaum and the bodies of three soldiers. Among those released were terrorists who returned to terror activity.
⚠ The outcome:

According to data from Almagor (Terror Victims Association), 183 Israelis were murdered by released prisoners across all exchange deals combined.

Sources: Almagor Terror Victims Association | Ynet | Jerusalem Post

The Gaza Disengagement

2005 — Government of Ariel Sharon
Israel unilaterally evacuated all settlements in Gaza. 8,500 settlers were removed. The IDF withdrew completely. Hamas took over Gaza in 2007 and turned it into a military base.
⚠ The outcome:

22,570+ rockets fired at Israel since the disengagement. 5 wars: Cast Lead, Pillar of Defense, Protective Edge, Guardian of the Walls, Swords of Iron.

Sources: IDF data | ISA reports | Ynet | Wikipedia

Force Depletion Around Gaza

2020–2023 — Security establishment
The IDF reduced forces around the Gaza border to minimal levels. On October 7, there were only 767 soldiers facing ~3,000 terrorists who physically breached the fence (out of ~6,000 mobilized). The working assumption: "The fence stops them, Hamas is deterred."
⚠ The outcome:

A force ratio of 1:4 on the day of the invasion. Soldiers fought heroically but could not stop ~3,000 terrorists who broke through.

Sources: State Comptroller Report | Commission of Inquiry | Channel 12

Rejection of the "Jericho Wall" Plan

2016–2023 — Security establishment
"Jericho Wall" was a detailed intelligence document that described Hamas's attack plan with precision: breaching the fence, infiltrating communities, kidnapping civilians. It was dismissed as a "fantasy scenario" and "too ambitious for Hamas."
⚠ The outcome:

On October 7, Hamas executed exactly what the document described — step by step.

Sources: New York Times investigation | Military investigations and State Comptroller reports | Channel 13

Closure of the IDF's Arabic Department

2000–2020 — Security establishment
The IDF dramatically reduced Arabic language studies, cultural research, and human intelligence (HUMINT) in Gaza. Intelligence budgets were shifted to technology (cyber, AI) at the expense of understanding the enemy.
⚠ The outcome:

A sharp decline in the ability to read the enemy, understand its intentions, and decipher warnings. This contributed to the intelligence failure of October 7.

Sources: Internal intelligence reports | Commission of Inquiry | Ynet

🗑 Deep Analysis: The Oslo Accords

Analysis of the concessions, promises, and actual outcomes of the Oslo process

What Israel Gave — and What It Received

Oslo A (1993) + Oslo B (1995)
Israel gave:
• Recognition of the PLO as the representative of the Palestinians
• Brought Arafat back from exile in Tunis to the territories
• Withdrawal from Palestinian cities (Area A)
• Arming the PA: 15,000 automatic rifles, 240 heavy machine guns, 45 armored vehicles
• A police force of 30,000-50,000 armed officers

Israel received:
• A letter from Arafat "renouncing terror" — which he violated repeatedly
• A promise of security cooperation — which collapsed during the Intifada
• 1,137 dead Israelis in the Second Intifada
⚠ Summary:

Israel made irreversible concessions (territory, weapons, legitimacy) and received promises in return that were not kept.

Sources: Oslo Accords | Britannica | Seton Hall University | THINC Israel

The Karine A Ship — 50 Tons of Iranian Weapons for Arafat

January 3, 2002 — During an active "peace process"
"I don't know about any ship" — Yasser Arafat (a documented lie)
Shayetet 13 (Israeli Navy commandos) captured the Karine A in the Red Sea, loaded with 50 tons of Iranian weapons: Katyusha rockets, anti-tank missiles, explosives, mortars, sniper rifles, mines, RPGs. The weapons were packed in 80 wooden crates inside waterproof containers manufactured only in Iran. The ship's captain: Colonel Omar Akawi — a Fatah operative and PA police member. The ship was captured while an American general was meeting Arafat to advance peace.
⚠ The significance:

While an active peace process was underway, the Palestinian Authority was importing 50 tons of weapons from Iran — indicating a gap between official declarations and actions on the ground.

Sources: Washington Institute | Jewish Virtual Library | Meir Amit Intelligence Center

Arafat Rejects Everything — Camp David and Clinton Parameters

July 2000 + December 2000
What Ehud Barak offered at Camp David (July 2000):
• A Palestinian state on ~73% of the West Bank in the initial offer + 100% of Gaza
• East Jerusalem as the Palestinian capital
• Sovereignty over Muslim and Christian quarters
• "Custodianship" over the Temple Mount

Clinton Parameters (December 2000) — even more generous:
• 94-96% of the West Bank + land swaps = ~97%
• A sovereign Palestinian capital in East Jerusalem
• An international compensation fund for refugees

Arafat rejected both offers and did not present a counter-proposal.
⚠ Documented responses:

Clinton to Arafat: "I am a colossal failure, and you made me one." Dennis Ross: "Arafat could not end the conflict because ending the conflict means ending himself."

Sources: Newsweek | Jewish Virtual Library | HonestReporting | Wikipedia

⚠ The finding:

Documentation reveals a consistent gap between Arafat's English statements (peace) and his Arabic speeches (jihad), while simultaneously receiving weapons, territory, and money from Israel.

Sources: MEMRI | Palestinian Media Watch | Middle East Forum | Wikipedia

Major attacks:

Sources: Wikipedia | ICT | B'Tselem Statistics

Weapons supplied to the Palestinian Authority under peace agreements were ultimately used against Israelis, and Israel was forced to conduct a military operation to confiscate the weapons it had itself provided.

🚫 Deep Analysis: The Gaza Disengagement

Analysis of the Gaza withdrawal (2005) — from promises to actual outcomes

"The disengagement is formaldehyde" — Weisglass's admission

Dov Weisglass, Sharon's adviser | Haaretz, October 2004
"The significance of the disengagement plan is the freezing of the peace process... The disengagement is actually formaldehyde. It supplies the amount of formaldehyde necessary to prevent a political process with the Palestinians."
Sharon's senior adviser openly admitted that the disengagement was not designed for peace but rather to freeze the process: "When you freeze the process, you prevent the establishment of a Palestinian state, and you prevent discussion of refugees, borders, and Jerusalem." Sharon himself was facing corruption investigations (the Greek Island Affair) — critics claimed the disengagement was meant to divert attention.
Sources: Haaretz | Wikipedia | Times of Israel

8,600 Evacuees — 21 Communities Destroyed

August 15-22, 2005
1,800 families were uprooted from 21 communities in Gaza + 4 in northern Samaria. 2,800 homes were demolished. 26 synagogues were destroyed.

The human cost:
• 40%+ of evacuees met criteria for PTSD (Ben-Gurion University study)
• Sharp increase in depression, heart disease, diabetes
• Suicides, divorces, poverty
• 10 years later: 350 families still in temporary caravans
• 11 years later: 13% still in temporary housing
⚠ The human impact:

1,800 families were evacuated from their homes. Academic research found high PTSD rates, and years later some evacuees were still not fully rehabilitated.

Sources: Jewish Action | Times of Israel | Ynetnews | Ben-Gurion University

$14 Million Greenhouses — Looted Within Hours

September 12, 2005 — The moment the last soldier left
An international foundation led by James Wolfensohn (former World Bank president) purchased the Gush Katif greenhouses for $14 million for Palestinian workers. Wolfensohn donated $500,000 from his own pocket. 800 of ~1,000 greenhouses were looted and destroyed the moment the army left. Simultaneously — synagogues were set ablaze by Palestinian mobs. At least 4 synagogues were burned in Netzarim, Kfar Darom, and Morag.
⚠ What actually happened:

Economic infrastructure worth $14 million, intended to employ Palestinian workers, was looted and destroyed within hours of the withdrawal. Synagogues were set on fire.

Sources: NPR | ASIL | Christian Century

Hamas Takes Over — From Elections to Coup

January 2006 — June 2007
• January 25, 2006: Hamas wins elections — 74 of 132 seats, 42.9% of the vote
• March 29, 2006: Hamas government led by Ismail Haniyeh
• June 14, 2007: Hamas seizes power by force — murders Fatah members, throws rivals off rooftops
• The PA is split: Hamas controls Gaza, Fatah controls the West Bank
⚠ The consequences:

Hamas exploited the Israeli withdrawal to win elections, then seized power by force in Gaza and built military capabilities for 16 years.

Sources: Wikipedia | Times of Israel

The Philadelphi Corridor — The Smuggling Highway

2005–2023 | 14 km of the Gaza-Egypt border
Israel withdrew from the Philadelphi Corridor. For 17 years it served as Hamas's lifeline. The Egyptian army destroyed 1,659 smuggling tunnels. Some were large enough for trucks. In 2005-2008 alone:

• 250 tons of explosives
• 80 tons of fertilizer (for bomb-making)
• 4,000 RPGs
• 1,800 rockets
• Iranian Fajr-5 missiles (75 km range)

Egyptian police in Sinai received bribes from Hamas in exchange for allowing weapons through.
⚠ The connection to October 7:

The withdrawal from the Philadelphi Corridor enabled massive weapons smuggling from Iran to Hamas. The data indicates that relinquishing control over this corridor contributed to Hamas's armament.

Sources: AIPAC | Washington Institute | FDD | HonestReporting

The chain of causation: From the Jibril deal (1985) through the Shalit deal (2011) to October 7

The Jibril Deal (1985) — The Background to Hamas's Founding

May 21, 1985 | Government of Shimon Peres
Israel released 1,150 security prisoners (including murderers sentenced to life) in exchange for 3 soldiers.

Who was released:
• Sheikh Ahmed Yassin — leader of the Muslim Brotherhood in Gaza. Sentenced in 1984 to 13 years for weapons stockpiling. Two years after his release — he founded Hamas (December 10, 1987)
• Kozo Okamoto — perpetrator of the Lod Airport massacre (1972, 26 killed). Sentenced to life imprisonment
⚠ Chain of causation:

Israel released Yassin (1985), Yassin founded Hamas (1987), Hamas carried out the October 7 attack (2023).

Sources: Wikipedia | Washington Institute | Middle East Monitor

The Tennenbaum Deal (2004) — Release of Samir Kuntar

January 29, 2004 + July 16, 2008
2004: Israel released 436 prisoners + 60 bodies in exchange for Elhanan Tennenbaum (a civilian kidnapped in a drug deal) + bodies of 3 soldiers.

2008: Israel released Samir Kuntar + 4 terrorists + 199 bodies in exchange for 2 coffins (Goldwasser and Regev, killed during kidnapping).

Who was Samir Kuntar?
April 22, 1979: He invaded Nahariya. Took Danny Haran (31) and his daughter Einat (4) hostage. Shot Danny in front of his daughter, then drowned him. Smashed 4-year-old Einat's skull with a rifle butt. Yael (2) died when her mother accidentally smothered her crying in hiding.
Sentenced to 4 life sentences + 47 years.
⚠ What happened next:

Kuntar was received as a hero in Lebanon, joined Hezbollah, and was killed in an airstrike in Syria (December 19, 2015).

Sources: Wikipedia | Ynetnews | Times of Israel | CBS News

The Shalit Deal (2011) — Release of Sinwar

October 18, 2011 | 1,027 prisoners for one soldier
"The release of prisoners will only be achieved through the kidnapping of Jews and exchanging them" — Yahya Sinwar, on the day of his release, 2011
Israel released 1,027 prisoners (280 sentenced to life) in exchange for Gilad Shalit.

Released prisoners who killed again:

• Ahmed Najar — directed fire that killed Malachi Rosenfeld (25) in June 2015
• Osama Asaad — involved in the murder of Danny Gonen (25) in June 2015
⚠ The data:

According to Shin Bet data, 82% of released prisoners returned to armed activity. 6 Israelis were murdered by Shalit deal releasees by 2015. Sinwar declared on his release day his intention to kidnap again — and on October 7, 251 were kidnapped.

Sources: Wikipedia | Jerusalem Post | AJC | Times of Israel
