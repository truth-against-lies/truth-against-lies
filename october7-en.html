Background — Gaza Under Hamas (2007–2023)

The development of Hamas rule in the Gaza Strip and the militarization of the territory

Hamas Seizes Control of Gaza

In June 2007, Hamas carried out a military coup against the Palestinian Authority and seized control of the Gaza Strip by force. Since then, the territory has served as a base for military operations against Israel.

  • Population: Approximately 2.2 million residents, 365 km² — one of the most densely populated places on Earth
  • Median age: ~18. Unemployment: ~45%. International aid: 70%+ of families dependent on aid. 97% of water unfit for drinking

"Gaza Metro" Tunnel Network: 350–500 km of tunnels beneath the Strip — longer than the London Underground. Estimated investment of over one billion dollars. Each tunnel required ~6,000 tons of concrete. Used as command centers, weapons factories, intelligence headquarters, and hostage transfer routes. Hamas used landline telephones in the tunnels to avoid intelligence collection.

The Hamas Charter — What Is Written in Black and White

The Hamas Charter (1988) contains clauses explicitly calling for war against Jews:

  • Article 7: «The hour will not come until the Muslims fight the Jews and kill them, until the Jew hides behind stones and trees, and the stones and trees cry out: O Muslim, O servant of Allah, there is a Jew behind me — come and kill him»
  • Article 13: «There is no solution to the Palestinian issue except through jihad. Initiatives, proposals, and international conferences — are all a waste of time»
  • Article 32: Cites the "Protocols of the Elders of Zion" as a legitimate source

Note: In 2017, Hamas published a new "political document" presenting more moderate positions — but never annulled the original charter. Hamas leaders cited Article 7 even after 2017. The events of October 7 indicate the charter reflects operational policy.

Rocket Arsenal

3,000–5,000 rockets in the opening barrage of October 7. Rocket types included:

  • Qassam — locally manufactured
  • M-75, B-7 — medium range
  • Fajr-5 — Iranian-made
  • Anti-tank missiles — for use against armored vehicles

Iran's Role

$100M–$350M
Annual Iranian funding to Hamas
  • $100M–$350M per year (increased to $350 million in 2023)
  • 500 fighters trained by the Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps
  • Transfer of advanced rocket technology
  • Coordination of the "Axis of Resistance" — Hamas, Hezbollah, Houthis, Iraqi militias
  • $7 million/month additional budget for "confrontation preparations"
Sources: State Comptroller Report 2024 | State Commission of Inquiry | Shin Bet | IDF

"The Conception" — The Failure That Enabled the Attack

The flawed core assumption that enabled the intelligence surprise

Core assumption: Hamas prefers payments ($30M monthly from Qatar) over confrontation — and therefore "is incapable of" a large-scale attack. This assumption was accepted by the majority of the Israeli security establishment.

Warnings That Were Dismissed

  • An August 2022 document from Yahya Sinwar described the exact plan — classified as an "aspiration" rather than "operational"
  • "Jericho Wall" — a Hamas document describing the exact attack plan — was in the possession of Israeli intelligence

Sinwar Quotes — Leaked and From Interviews

Yahya Sinwar, the Hamas leader who planned the attack, expressed his intentions publicly and in internal communications:

  • Leaked encrypted messages (WSJ, 2024): Sinwar wrote to senior Hamas officials that civilian casualties in Gaza were «necessary sacrifices» and that «their deaths infuse life into the veins of this nation»
  • In the same messages, he noted the war was «generating global change in favor of the Palestinians» — and drew comparisons to Vietnam and Algeria
  • Sinwar was eliminated on October 16, 2024 by armored soldiers from Brigade 828 in Rafah. Found alone, wounded, in a destroyed building — not in a protected tunnel

Sinwar, who defined Gazan civilian casualties as «necessary sacrifices,» was found at the time of his elimination alone, without accompanying fighters.

The Nukhba Unit (Elite Commando)

Hamas commando fighters were specifically trained for every phase: tunnel warfare, fence breaching, infiltration of communities. They gathered detailed intelligence on every kibbutz — locations of safe rooms, weapons caches, guard shift schedules. Only 5 Hamas leaders knew the complete plan.

The Technological "Jericho Wall"

  • 65 km long, cost billions of shekels, completed in 2021
  • Sensors, cameras, remote positions, underground barrier at an estimated depth of 30 meters (per reports)
  • Breached at dozens of points simultaneously (up to 119 per later investigations) — the entire investment rendered meaningless
Sources: State Comptroller Report | Commission of Inquiry | Ynet | Haaretz

Minute-by-Minute Timeline — October 7, 2023

The sequence of events as they unfolded on October 7, 2023

Hamas fighters begin moving to staging positions along the fence

06:29

Massive rocket barrage: 3,000–5,000 rockets on southern and central Israel. Sirens across the country

06:30

Under rocket cover, Hamas breaches the fence at dozens of points simultaneously (up to 119 per later investigations). Bulldozers, explosives, vehicles, drones. Thousands of cameras and remote positions are disabled

06:39

3 terrorists on motorized paragliders land in Netiv HaAsara

06:42

Terrorists on motorcycles head toward Kfar Aza and Be'eri

06:50

Hamas fighters on pickup trucks breach Kfar Aza from two directions

06:56

First Hamas fighters arrive at Kibbutz Be'eri

06:58

40 Nukhba commandos enter Sderot

07:00

Nova Music Festival: Within minutes, hundreds of terrorists from motorcycles and pickup trucks surround the venue, shooting indiscriminately at ~3,500 attendees

07:00

20+ communities under simultaneous attack: Be'eri, Kfar Aza, Nir Oz, Kissufim, Nirim, Ein HaShlosha, Alumim, Holit, Sufa, Netiv HaAsara, Sderot, Ofakim, Re'im base, Nahal Oz, and more

07:00

Nahal Oz Observation Post (850 m from Gaza): Hamas breaches. Unit 414 observers hide in shelter — most were killed or taken hostage

07:40

IDF finally confirms terrorists have crossed into Israel — 1 hour and 10 minutes after the fence was breached

08:00

Brigadier General Hamami killed — one of the most senior officers. Gaza Division overwhelmed: 767 soldiers facing ~3,000 infiltrated terrorists (out of ~6,000 mobilized). IDF HQ in Tel Aviv unaware of the full scope. Entire communities under massacre with no military response

12:00

Reinforcements begin arriving. Only at noon does the Gaza Division begin functioning again

16:00

Maj. Gen. (Res.) Noam Tibon, after driving from Tel Aviv and fighting terrorists along the way, reaches his son's home in Nahal Oz and rescues his family

Evening

Evening of Oct 7 through morning of Oct 8 — IDF forces gradually regain control. Last battle at Sderot police station — ~24 hours after the attack

Sources: IDF — Official Timeline | State Comptroller Report 2024 | Fighter testimonies

Community-by-Community Breakdown

Analysis of the events in each of the attacked communities

132 killed (101 civilians + 31 security forces) | 32 taken hostage | 125 homes damaged/destroyed

  • 1,100 residents, approximately 4 km from Gaza
  • A Canadian-Israeli peace community that transported patients from Gaza to Israeli hospitals
  • 16 families lost 2 or more family members
  • Among the dead: peace activist Vivian Silver (74)
Sources: Israel Police | National Insurance | Survivor testimonies | Ynet | Haaretz

Kibbutz Kfar Aza

~80 killed (62 residents + 18 security forces) | 19 taken hostage

  • ~250 attackers (120–165 Nukhba + ~80 additional)
  • ~750 residents
  • The entire kibbutz was overrun. Considered one of the most severe sites of the event
Sources: Israel Police | National Insurance | Survivor testimonies | Ynet | Haaretz

Kibbutz Nir Oz

47–57 killed | 76 taken hostage (67 alive, 9 killed)

  • 500+ attackers and looters, ~400 residents, 2 km from Gaza
  • Suffered the highest proportional loss — approximately one-quarter of all residents were killed or taken hostage
  • Shiri Bibas with her two children on a motorcycle — one of the most widely documented images from the event
  • The Bibas family: Yarden (34), Shiri (32), Ariel (4), Kfir (9 months — the youngest hostage)
Sources: Israel Police | National Insurance | Survivor testimonies | Ynet | Haaretz

Nova Music Festival — Re'im

378 killed (344 civilians + 34 security forces) | 44 taken hostage

  • ~3,500 attendees, 5 km from Gaza, open area with no shelter
  • The deadliest single event against civilians in Israeli history
  • A group of ~30 people hid in a roadside bomb shelter — Hamas threw grenades in repeatedly
  • Hamas originally planned to attack the access roads, but upon discovering the festival redirected forces
  • Sexual violence at Nova: The Pramila Patten report (UN, March 2024) found «credible information» of rape and gang rape at the festival site. A survivor's testimony described gang rape, torture, and murder of a young woman near one of the shelters
  • 40+ were kidnapped from the festival to Gaza. According to testimonies, some were held under harsh conditions including violence
Sources: Israel Police | National Insurance | Survivor testimonies | Ynet | Haaretz | Pramila Patten Report (UN) March 2024

Nahal Oz Observation Post

  • Battle at the police station: Hamas seized the station. 30 police officers and civilians were killed there

Acts of Defense and Rescue

Extraordinary actions by civilians and security personnel during the event

Aner Shapira (22) — Nova Music Festival

Threw back 7 grenades that Hamas had hurled into the shelter at the Nova Festival. The 8th grenade exploded and killed him.

He saved 7 of the 27 people in the shelter. Unarmed, he stood at the shelter entrance and threw grenades back out.

Sources: IDF | Ministry of Defense | Family testimonies | Ynet | Times of Israel

Maj. Gen. (Res.) Noam Tibon

Drove from Tel Aviv with his personal pistol, fought terrorists along the way, reached his son's home in Nahal Oz and rescued his family.

Sources: IDF | Ministry of Defense | Family testimonies | Ynet | Times of Israel

Rachel Edri — "The Grandmother of Ofakim"

Found 5 Hamas terrorists in her home. Instead of panicking, she served them tea and cookies for hours, maintained conversation, and secretly alerted the police.

Sources: IDF | Ministry of Defense | Family testimonies | Ynet | Times of Israel

Inbal Rabin-Lieberman (26) — Nir Am

Security coordinator of Kibbutz Nir Am. At 06:30 when sirens sounded, she immediately armed her team and distributed weapons — a critical decision since in other communities the armory remained locked.

A team of 12 fought the terrorists and eliminated 25. They held their position for 3.5 hours until the IDF arrived.

Nir Am was the only community in the area where no resident was killed or taken hostage. However, Samer Fuad Talalka (24), a worker from the community, was kidnapped and killed by IDF fire on December 15, 2023.

Sources: IDF | Ministry of Defense | Family testimonies | Ynet | Times of Israel

Rami Davidian (59) — Nova Music Festival

The Hostage Crisis

Overview of the kidnapping data, captivity conditions, and release operations

251 people were taken hostage on October 7. Approximately 25 nationalities. Nearly half held dual citizenship.

Youngest: Kfir Bibas — 9 months. Oldest: 85+

Sources: Hostage Families Forum | IDF | Ynet | BBC | NY Times

Conditions of Captivity (From testimonies of released hostages)

  • Complete darkness, death threats, sexual violence (13 women and 1–2 men testified)
  • Forced propaganda filming
  • Tunnels less than 1.5 m high, 6 people crammed together
  • Sleeping near tunnel ventilation openings
  • One meal per day (pita or rice) or nothing
  • Hostages lost 30+ kg over months
  • Narrow tunnels: 2 sqm, height below standing
Sources: Hostage Families Forum | IDF | Ynet | BBC | NY Times

November 2023 Deal

  • 7-day ceasefire
  • Hamas released 105 civilian hostages (81 Israelis, 23 Thai nationals, 1 Filipino)
  • Israel released 240 Palestinian security prisoners (107 of them minors, most without convictions)
  • Mediated by Qatar

Military Rescue Operations

  • June 2024 — Operation "Arnon": 4 hostages rescued alive (Noa Argamani, Andrey Kozlov, Shlomi Ziv, Almog Meir Jan)
  • August 2024: Farhan al-Qadi (Bedouin) rescued from southern Gaza
  • February 2024 — "Golden Hand": 2 hostages rescued from Rafah (Fernando Marman and Louis Har)
Sources: Hostage Families Forum | IDF | Ynet | BBC | NY Times

Noa Argamani

Rescued June 8, 2024 after 245 days. Held in 6 different locations.

The footage of her kidnapping on a motorcycle, in which she can be heard crying "Don't kill me!" to her boyfriend Or (also taken hostage), became the most widely recognized image of October 7.

Sources: Hostage Families Forum | IDF | Ynet | BBC | NY Times

The Bibas Family Tragedy

  • The IDF determined that Ariel (4) and Kfir (9 months) were murdered by their captors in November 2023 "in cold blood, with bare hands"
  • Hamas claimed they were killed in an Israeli airstrike — a lie that was disproven
  • February 2025: Hamas returned coffins — confirmed the bodies of Ariel and Kfir, but the third body was not Shiri's — Hamas attempted to deceive with an unidentified woman's body from Gaza
  • Shiri's body was returned later. Funeral held on February 26, 2025 — tens of thousands of Israelis lined the route
  • Yarden Bibas (the father) was released after 484 days
  • Kfir, kidnapped at 9 months old, was the youngest hostage in history

⚠ Summary of the Bibas Family Case:

According to IDF findings, Hamas kidnapped a family with a 9-month-old baby, murdered the children, provided false information about the circumstances of their death, and attempted to return a body that was not the mother's.

Sources: Hostage Families Forum | IDF | Ynet | BBC | NY Times

January 2025 Deal — Phase 1

  • 33 hostages returned
  • Israel released ~2,000 Palestinian prisoners
  • 5 Thai hostages also returned
  • Israel acknowledged that 8 of the returned hostages were already dead
  • 42-day ceasefire

Conclusion: By October 13, 2025 — the last 20 living hostages were released.

Last hostage: Ran Gavili, whose body was returned on January 26, 2026.

Total returned alive: 168. Bodies returned: 85

Sources: Hostage Families Forum | IDF | Ynet | BBC | NY Times

Sexual Violence — UN Findings

Findings from the Pramila Patten report, the Special Representative on Sexual Violence in Conflict, and international responses

Pramila Patten Report — UN Mission Findings

(Source: Chapter 3, «The Inner Faces of Israel» — Nathanael Linder)

The mission visited Israel January-February 2024. Conducted 33 meetings with Israeli survivors, examined 5,000+ photographs, watched 50 hours of video, and held 34 classified interviews.

Key Findings

  • Rape and gang rape at a minimum of 3 locations: Nova Festival, Road 232, kibbutzim
  • «Credible information and testimony» that hostages in Gaza were sexually assaulted
  • «There are grounds to believe that sexual violence may still be occurring» (against hostages)
  • Systematic sexual humiliation methods were documented
  • Pathologists and witnesses described: torn clothing, signs of sexual violence and rape
  • Testimonies of gang rape of women who were murdered during or after the act
  • 13 women and 1-2 men reported experiences or evidence of sexual violence (Source: Project Dinah Report, 2025)
Sources: Pramila Patten Report (UN) March 2024 | Project Dinah Report (2025) | ZAKA | Israel Police

Response of Women's Rights Organizations

Notably, some women's rights organizations responded with significant delay. International women's organizations took 50 days to issue a statement (until December 1, 2023). Simultaneously, social media campaigns questioned the testimonies.

This phenomenon has been described in the literature as «traumatic erasure» — ignoring the experiences of victims.

In response, Danielle Ofek and Natalie Livski created the hashtag #MeToo_UNless_UR_a_Jew which gathered approximately 300,000 signatures from 50 countries demanding that UN Women condemn the attacks.

The gap between the principle of «believe women» and the actual response drew criticism of gender selectivity.

Sources: Pramila Patten Report (UN) March 2024 | ZAKA | Israel Police

Intelligence Failure — Comparison to 1973

Analysis of the intelligence failure and comparison to the Yom Kippur War of 1973

Warnings That Were Dismissed

  • Unit 8200 dismissed warnings — Observers reported unusual Hamas training exercises, tunnel breach drills, movement patterns. Reports were dismissed as "noise"
  • At least once, an observer was warned in a military briefing that continued reporting of "nonsense" about Hamas attacks would have consequences
  • Intelligence had the exact plan since at least 2022. Analysts who argued that Hamas capabilities and intentions were changing — their assessments were rejected
  • The "Jericho Wall" document from Hamas — describing the exact attack plan — was classified as an "aspiration"

The technological defense system did not withstand the test: Hamas breached at dozens of points simultaneously. Cameras were disabled. The underground barrier was irrelevant — Hamas breached above ground, not below it.

Sources: State Comptroller Report | Agranat Commission (1973) | Winograd Commission (2006)

Global Response + War in Gaza

What happened next — a brief overview

  • Russia: Issued a "warning" statement
  • China: Called for "restraint"
  • Guterres condemned but said "it did not happen in a vacuum" — a statement that angered Israel

    • 9 UNRWA employees were fired by the UN itself after an internal investigation found they had participated in October 7

    • 16 countries froze funding to UNRWA in response (including the USA, UK, Germany, Australia, and others)
    • Tunnels were found beneath UNRWA facilities and Hamas command centers adjacent to UNRWA schools

The KIDNAPPED Campaign

Posters were distributed in New York, London, Paris, and Berlin. The phenomenon of tearing down posters was widely documented — ADL documented 57+ incidents on American campuses.

Al-Ahli Hospital Incident (October 17): Initial reports claimed ~500 dead, blamed Israel.

Reality: An explosion in a parking lot from a failed Palestinian rocket. Independent investigations (including HRW) confirmed — most likely a Palestinian Islamic Jihad rocket. The death toll was significantly lower.

Sources: Ministry of Foreign Affairs | Reuters | AP | BBC | HRW | ADL

Operation "Swords of Iron" — Overview

  • October 2023: Declaration of war, aerial bombardment

  • May 2024: Rafah operation
  • June 2024: 4 hostages rescued in Nuseirat

100,000+ ground troops at the operation's peak

Tunnel Warfare — "Gaza Metro"

Some tunnels descended to depths of 40–60 meters. The IDF discovered and neutralized hundreds of kilometers of tunnels beneath hospitals, schools, UNRWA facilities, and mosques.

  • Scope: Estimates of 350–500 km of tunnels beneath the Strip — longer than the London Underground (402 km)
  • Cost: Estimated investment of over one billion dollars — international aid funds diverted to terror infrastructure
  • Materials: Approximately 6,000 tons of concrete for the entire tunnel network (IDF Intelligence). UNRWA and USAID cement bags were found inside the tunnels
  • Purpose: Command posts, weapons depots, rocket factories, hostage transfer routes, connections to hospitals and schools used as human shields

International Military Experts

  • Colonel Richard Kemp (former commander of British forces in Afghanistan): «The IDF does more than any military in history to minimize civilian casualties»
  • John Spencer (Chair of Urban Warfare Research, West Point): «A combatant-to-civilian ratio of 1:1.5 is unprecedented in urban warfare» — the historical average is 1:9
