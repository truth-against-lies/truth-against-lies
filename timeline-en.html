The Age of Enlightenment — 1807-1880

1807ZionismNapoleon's Sanhedrin▼

Napoleon convened 71 Jewish leaders in Paris as a "Grand Sanhedrin" to regulate the status of Jews in the French Empire. A precedent for Jewish emancipation in Europe.

Source: Encyclopedia Judaica, "Napoleon and the Jews"
1840AntisemitismThe Damascus Affair▼

A Capuchin monk and his servant disappeared in the Jewish quarter of Damascus. Jews were accused of "ritual murder" and tortured until they "confessed." Moses Montefiore and Adolphe Cremieux worked to free the detainees.

Source: Jonathan Frankel, The Damascus Affair (1997)
1858AntisemitismThe Mortara Affair▼

Edgardo Mortara, a 6-year-old Jewish boy from Bologna, was seized by order of the Pope after a Christian servant secretly baptized him. The affair shocked Europe and strengthened calls for an international Jewish organization.

Source: David Kertzer, The Kidnapping of Edgardo Mortara (1997)
1860AchievementsFounding of Alliance Israelite Universelle▼

The first international Jewish organization was founded in Paris. Its mission was to protect Jewish rights worldwide and establish schools in the Middle East and North Africa.

Source: Andre Kaspi, Histoire de l'Alliance Israelite Universelle
1862ZionismMoses Hess — "Rome and Jerusalem"▼

Socialist philosopher Moses Hess published "Rome and Jerusalem" — one of the first proto-Zionist books arguing for national revival in the Land of Israel.

Source: Shlomo Avineri, Moses Hess: Prophet of Communism and Zionism
1870ZionismMikveh Israel — First Agricultural School▼

The "Mikveh Israel" agricultural school was established near Jaffa — the first modern Jewish educational institution in the Land of Israel.

Source: Jewish Virtual Library
1878ZionismFounding of Petah Tikva▼

Petah Tikva, the "Mother of Colonies," was established as the first Jewish agricultural colony in the Land of Israel.

Source: Encyclopedia Judaica, "Petah Tikvah"

The Rise of Zionism — 1881-1917

1881AntisemitismPogroms Begin in Russia▼

A wave of violent pogroms erupted in southern Russia after the assassination of Tsar Alexander II. About 2 million Jews left Russia by 1914.

Source: John Klier, Russians, Jews, and the Pogroms of 1881-1882
1882ZionismThe First Aliyah▼

About 25,000-35,000 Jews immigrated to the Land of Israel, mainly from Russia and Romania. They established colonies such as Rishon LeZion, Zikhron Ya'akov, and Rehovot.

Source: Encyclopedia Judaica, "Aliyah"
1894AntisemitismThe Dreyfus Affair▼

Captain Alfred Dreyfus, a French-Jewish officer, was falsely accused of spying for Germany. The affair exposed deep antisemitism in France and inspired Herzl to write "The Jewish State."

Source: Britannica, "Dreyfus Affair" | Ruth Harris, Dreyfus
1896ZionismHerzl — "The Jewish State"▼

Theodor Herzl published "Der Judenstaat" — arguing that the only solution to antisemitism was the establishment of a sovereign Jewish state. The founding text of political Zionism.

Source: Theodor Herzl, Der Judenstaat (1896)
1897ZionismThe First Zionist Congress▼

208 delegates from 17 countries convened in Basel, Switzerland. The Congress founded the World Zionist Organization. Herzl wrote: "In Basel I founded the Jewish State."

Source: Central Zionist Archives
1903AntisemitismThe Kishinev Pogrom▼

A violent pogrom in Kishinev (present-day Moldova). 49 Jews murdered, hundreds wounded, 1,500 homes damaged. Bialik wrote "In the City of Slaughter."

49 killed, hundreds wounded

Source: Steven Zipperstein, Pogrom: Kishinev and the Tilt of History (2018)
1903AntisemitismPublication of "The Protocols of the Elders of Zion"▼

The most infamous antisemitic forgery in history. Proven a fabrication as early as 1921 by The Times, yet continues to spread hatred to this day.

Source: Norman Cohn, Warrant for Genocide (1967)
1904ZionismThe Second Aliyah▼

About 35,000-40,000 Jews immigrated (1904-1914), mainly young socialists from Russia. They established the kibbutz movement and the social infrastructure of the Yishuv. Ben-Gurion arrived in this wave.

Source: Encyclopedia Judaica, "Second Aliyah"
1909AchievementsFounding of Tel Aviv and Degania▼

66 Jewish families drew lots for plots on sand dunes north of Jaffa — thus Tel Aviv was born. Around the same time, Degania, the first kibbutz, was established on the Sea of Galilee.

Source: Maoz Azaryahu, Tel Aviv: Mythography of a City
1913AntisemitismThe Beilis Trial▼

Menahem Mendel Beilis was accused of murdering a Christian boy — a classic blood libel. After a sensational trial, he was acquitted, but it exposed the depth of antisemitism in Tsarist Russia.

Source: Edmund Levin, A Child of Christian Blood (2014)
2.11.1917ZionismThe Balfour Declaration▼

British Foreign Secretary Arthur Balfour: "His Majesty's Government view with favour the establishment in Palestine of a national home for the Jewish people." The first international legal basis for Jewish rights in the Land of Israel.

Source: Jonathan Schneer, The Balfour Declaration (2010)
