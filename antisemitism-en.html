What Is Antisemitism?

The oldest hatred in human history — 2,500 years of lies that need to be exposed

Last updated: March 2026

TL;DR
  • Antisemitism = hatred of Jews spanning 2,500 years — the IHRA definition is recognized by 47 countries
  • 34 antisemitic libels debunked with facts and academic sources
  • 8 categories: religious, racial, economic, conspiracy, cultural, anti-Zionist, Holocaust denial, online
  • Each claim includes a factual refutation + the psychology behind the lie + sources

A Hatred That Changes Shape

Antisemitism is the most persistent group hatred in human history. It has taken different forms in different eras — religious, racial, political, economic, conspiratorial — but the core pattern remains the same: Jews are turned into scapegoats for society's problems.

Why Documentation Matters

Confronting antisemitism requires a deep understanding of its mechanisms. This site presents 34 common antisemitic claims across 8 categories, each with factual analysis, psychological context, and academic sources.

Three Layers of Response

1. Factual Analysis — examining the claim against the evidence
2. Psychological Context — cognitive mechanisms explaining the claim's spread
3. Historical Context — documented consequences of the claim

Religious and Theological Antisemitism

The oldest layer: hatred rooted in religious narratives

"The Jews killed Jesus"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Historical evidence indicates that Jesus was executed by crucifixion — a distinctly Roman method of capital punishment — on the orders of the Roman governor Pontius Pilate. The canonical Gospels were written 40–70 years after the events they describe, during a period of separation between emerging Christianity and Judaism. The Second Vatican Council (1965) officially stated, in the document Nostra Aetate, that collective guilt for the crucifixion cannot be attributed to the Jewish people as a whole.

Psychological Context

The spread of this claim can be attributed to the scapegoating mechanism combined with emotion-driven reasoning. Early Christians needed an explanation for why Jews — Jesus's own people — did not accept his messianic claim. Blaming the Jews provided an accessible emotional answer. Additionally, during a period when Christianity sought legitimacy from the Roman Empire, directing blame at Rome was politically dangerous, so it was redirected toward Jews as a convenient alternative target.

Sources
  1. Raymond Brown, The Death of the Messiah (1994)
  2. Vatican II, Nostra Aetate (1965) — "What happened in the passion of Jesus cannot be charged against all the Jews, without distinction, then alive, nor against the Jews of today"
  3. Vatican Commission for Religious Relations with the Jews, The Gifts and the Calling of God Are Irrevocable (2015) — rejected supersessionism and affirmed that the covenant with the Jews was never revoked

"Jews are children of the Devil"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The verse in John 8:44 reflects an intra-Jewish debate in the specific context of the first century. Jesus himself was Jewish, his disciples were Jewish, and early Christianity developed as an intra-Jewish movement. Applying one side of an internal debate to the entire ethno-religious group across generations constitutes a fundamental hermeneutical error — analogous to drawing conclusions about an entire nation from a single internal dispute.

Psychological Context

The central psychological mechanism is dehumanization. According to research, labeling an out-group as satanic or subhuman is a documented precursor to mass violence. Gregory Stanton's Ten Stages model identifies dehumanization as Stage 4 in the process leading to genocide (8 stages in 1987, presented to the U.S. State Department in 1996, expanded to 10 in 2012). The demonization of Jews served as a basis for legitimizing pogroms for centuries.

Sources
  1. Adele Reinhartz (2018)
  2. Gregory Stanton, The 8 Stages of Genocide (1987, presented to State Dept 1996, expanded to 10 in 2012)

"Blood libel: Jews use the blood of Christian children in rituals"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

In 900 years since the first documented blood libel (Norwich, 1144), not a single case has been proven. Historian Walter Laqueur counted approximately 150 documented cases of blood libel (alongside thousands of rumors) that led to arrests and killings of Jews. Jewish law explicitly prohibits the consumption of blood: "You shall not eat the blood of any flesh, for the life of all flesh is its blood" (Leviticus 17:14). Kashrut laws require complete draining of blood from meat before consumption. Pope Innocent IV (1247) and Emperor Frederick II (1236) investigated the accusations and found them without factual basis.

The Damascus Affair (1840): A Franciscan friar disappeared, and the Jewish community was accused of ritual murder. Eight community leaders, including the chief rabbi, were arrested and tortured — 4 died. Sir Moses Montefiore (Britain) and Adolphe Crémieux (France) campaigned for the prisoners' release. Ottoman Sultan Abdulmejid I issued a firman prohibiting the blood libel. The Beilis Trial (1911–1913): Menahem Mendel Beilis, a Jewish manager in Kyiv, was accused of ritual murder and imprisoned for two years — acquitted by an entirely Christian jury. Even within the legal framework of Tsarist Russia, when evidence was presented, the blood libel was rejected.

It should be noted that the blood libel continues to appear in contemporary media: the Syrian television series "Al-Shatat" (2003) depicted blood libel scenes and was broadcast on Al-Manar (Hezbollah). In 2013, an Egyptian politician declared on live broadcast that Jews "slaughter a Christian child on Passover." These findings indicate that the libel is not merely a historical phenomenon but an active component of contemporary propaganda.

Psychological Context

The spread of the libel can be attributed to two documented psychological mechanisms: projection and blame displacement. The blood libel emerged during the Crusades, when Christians themselves carried out mass violence. Accusing Jews of blood rituals functioned as psychological projection — transferring guilt for Christian violence onto the Jewish out-group. Additionally, the accusation provided justification for seizing Jewish property and expelling Jewish populations, indicating an economic-social motive alongside the psychological mechanism.

Sources

  2. Magda Teter, Blood Libel (2020)
  3. Ronald Florence, Blood Libel: The Damascus Affair of 1840 (2004)
  4. USHMM Holocaust Encyclopedia — Blood Libel
  5. Times of Israel (2013) — Egyptian politician revives Passover blood libel

"Host desecration: Jews stab communion wafers"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The accusation contains an internal contradiction: it simultaneously requires the assumption that Jews reject the Christian doctrine of transubstantiation (the belief that the wafer becomes the body of Christ) and that they attack the wafer out of recognition of its sanctity. Logically, if Jews do not believe the wafer is Jesus, they have no motivation to harm it. Historian Miri Rubin documented that host desecration accusations overlapped with periods of economic tension between Jewish and Christian communities, indicating that economic competition, not theology, was the primary driver. In the Rintfleisch massacres (1298), 146 Jewish communities in Germany were destroyed on the basis of these accusations.

Psychological Context

The evidence points to a combination of attribution error and economic scapegoating. Communities experiencing economic distress needed justification for seizing Jewish assets, and religious accusation provided moral legitimacy for economic action. This pattern — using moral or religious accusations to justify material gain — is a recurring motif throughout the history of antisemitism.

Sources
  1. Miri Rubin, Gentile Tales: The Narrative Assault on Late Medieval Jews (1999)

"Jews poison wells and spread plagues"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Historical evidence indicates that Jews were not immune to the Black Death — high mortality rates were recorded in Jewish communities as well. In some communities, Jewish mortality rates were slightly lower, a phenomenon historians attribute to frequent handwashing (as required by Jewish law), quarantine of the sick, and separate water sources in ghettos — religious-hygienic practices, not conspiracy. Pope Clement VI issued two papal bulls in 1348 to protect Jews, noting that Jews were affected by the plague like the rest of the population. The massacres continued despite this declaration.

Psychological Context

According to Terror Management Theory (Greenberg, Solomon & Pyszczynski), when people confront death anxiety, they tend toward heightened hostility against out-groups. The Black Death killed 30–60% of Europe's population within 4 years. Blaming Jews for the plague transformed a random disaster into an event with an identifiable causal agent, thereby restoring a perceived sense of control.

Sources
  1. Samuel Cohn, The Black Death and the Burning of Jews (2007)
  2. Pope Clement VI, Bull Quamvis Perfidiam (1348)
  3. Allington, Hirsh & Katz, Correlation between coronavirus conspiracism and antisemitism, Nature Scientific Reports (2023) — significant correlation between COVID conspiracies and antisemitism

"Jews are cursed to wander without a homeland"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The Jewish exile from the Land of Israel was the result of documented military conquests: the Babylonian Exile (586 BCE), the destruction of the Temple by Rome (70 CE), and the Bar Kokhba revolt (135 CE). Subsequently, Jews were expelled from countries by sovereign decree: England (1290), France (1306, 1394), Spain (1492), Portugal (1497). Each expulsion was carried out on the orders of rulers for documented political and economic reasons. Framing forced uprooting as a "curse" constitutes a clear example of victim blaming.

Psychological Context

According to the Just-World Hypothesis (Melvin Lerner, 1980), humans have a documented psychological need to believe that the world is fair and that people get what they deserve. When Jews were expelled and persecuted, observers tended to attribute the persecution to the victims' own actions — a cognitive mechanism that allows preservation of belief in an orderly moral world.

Sources

  2. David Nirenberg, Anti-Judaism: The Western Tradition (2013)

"The Talmud contains hate teachings against non-Jews"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The Talmud is a corpus of 6,200 pages of legal-juridical discussions spanning approximately 300 years (200–500 CE). It contains diverse opinions on every topic, often contradictory, since its purpose is to document discussion and debate, not establish a single dogmatic position. Extracting isolated passages without their legal and literary context is methodologically equivalent to quoting a minority opinion from a court ruling and claiming it represents the law.

The Talmud explicitly states: "The righteous of all nations have a share in the World to Come" (Tosefta Sanhedrin 13:2). The principle of "dina d'malkhuta dina" (the law of the land is the law) requires honesty in business with all people. The principle of pikuach nefesh (saving life) overrides nearly all commandments and requires the saving of all human life without distinction.

Psychological Context

The central psychological mechanisms are confirmation bias and cherry-picking. People with a preexisting belief that Jews are hostile to non-Jews search the Talmud for passages confirming their position, ignore thousands of contradicting passages, and strip context from those they select. Applying this method to any religious text — the Bible, the Quran, Hindu scriptures — would yield similar results of ostensible incitement to violence.

Sources

  2. David Berger, Persecution, Polemic, and Dialogue (2010)

Racial and Biological Antisemitism

Pseudo-scientific hatred from the 19th and 20th centuries

▼ Click to expand

"Jews are a separate and inferior race"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Findings from the Human Genome Project (2003) showed that genetic variation within any ethnic group exceeds the variation between groups. Humans share 99.9% of their DNA regardless of origin. Jewish populations exhibit significant genetic diversity: Ashkenazi, Sephardic, Mizrahi, Ethiopian, Indian, and Chinese Jews differ from one another substantially. The term "Jewish race" has no scientific-genetic validity.

Psychological Context

According to Social Dominance Theory (Sidanius & Pratto, 1999), some people have a documented tendency to organize groups into value hierarchies. Racial classification provided a pseudo-scientific framework for ranking human groups, thereby lending an appearance of rationality and evidential basis to prejudices.

Sources
  1. Human Genome Project (2003)
  2. UNESCO, Statement on Race (1950-1967)

"Jews are genetically predisposed to greed and manipulation"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Research in behavioral genetics consistently indicates that complex behavioral traits (personality, moral character, financial behavior) are shaped by a combination of genetics and environment, not by membership in an ethnic group. No identified "greed gene" exists. Studies on charitable giving patterns consistently show that Jewish communities are among the most generous in the world. The association between Jews and money is a historical-cultural construction: medieval Church laws restricted Jews to moneylending, and Jews were then accused for the consequences of the restriction imposed upon them.

Psychological Context

The central mechanism is the Fundamental Attribution Error (Lee Ross, 1977) — a cognitive bias causing people to attribute others' behavior to internal character traits rather than situational factors. When Jews engaged in interest-bearing lending, observers attributed this to a perceived Jewish character rather than structural constraints (exclusion from other professions). Logically, this attribution is equivalent to blaming a wheelchair user for not using the stairs.

Sources
  1. Lee Ross (1977)
  2. Steven Pinker, The Blank Slate (2002)

"Jews are subhuman (Untermenschen)"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Classifying a human group as "subhuman" is identified in research as a propaganda technique, not a scientific statement. Gregory Stanton's Ten Stages model identifies dehumanization as Stage 4 in the process leading to genocide (8 stages in 1987, presented to the State Department in 1996, expanded to 10 in 2012) — the stage that psychologically enables the act of killing. It is worth noting the logical contradiction in Nazi propaganda, which simultaneously claimed that Jews were subhuman and that they secretly ruled the world. Umberto Eco identified this contradiction as a defining feature of fascist ideology: "The enemy is both too strong and too weak."

Psychological Context

According to Moral Disengagement theory (Albert Bandura, 1999), ordinary people are capable of committing acts of cruelty when their moral regulatory mechanisms are neutralized. Dehumanization is identified as the most effective disengagement mechanism: when victims are reclassified as non-human, perpetrators can harm them without the psychological cost of guilt. The Holocaust required the participation or silent consent of millions of German citizens. Systematic dehumanization made this process psychologically possible.

"Jews promote racial mixing to weaken other races"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

From a historical perspective, the accusation constitutes a clear example of projection. The Nazi regime itself implemented eugenics programs on a massive scale: the T4 program caused the deaths of over 70,000 people with disabilities, the Lebensborn program promoted selective breeding, forced sterilizations were carried out on approximately 400,000 people, and the Holocaust claimed the lives of 6 million Jews and millions of others. Accusing Jews of acts that the accusers themselves committed constitutes classic projection. Contemporary versions, such as the "Great Replacement" theory, claim that Jews orchestrate mass immigration. In reality, migration movements are driven by structural factors: economic forces, armed conflicts, climate change, and policies of democratic governments with diverse leadership.

Psychological Context

The psychological mechanism is institutional projection — when an entire regime attributes its own crimes to its victims. This mechanism serves two documented purposes: (1) justifying the regime's actions ("we must act this way because they are acting against us"), and (2) immunizing the population against empathy for victims ("they would do worse to us if they could"). This is a recognized and documented propaganda strategy.

Economic and Financial Stereotypes

The money myth: accusations of greed, control, and exploitation

"Jews control the banks and global financial systems"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

In medieval Europe, Jews were excluded from most professions (agriculture, guilds, government). One of the few occupations permitted to them was moneylending, which the Christian Church prohibited for its believers. When Jewish lenders succeeded, they became targets of hostility for the success that was forced upon them. The Rothschild family (founded in the 1760s) became a symbol exploited by antisemites. In reality, Jews constitute approximately 0.2% of the world's population and do not control the global banking system.

According to S&P Global data (2025), the four largest banks in the world are Chinese state-owned: ICBC (approximately $6.3 trillion), Agricultural Bank of China, China Construction Bank, and Bank of China. Of the 20 largest banks — 7 are Chinese, and the rest are Japanese, British, French, and American. None of these banks is controlled by a Jewish family. The claim of "Jewish control of banking" does not withstand basic statistical scrutiny.

Psychological Context

The central psychological mechanism is the Illusory Correlation bias (Hamilton & Gifford, 1976): when a minority member is involved in a notable event, people tend to overestimate the connection between group membership and the event. When a Jewish banker is involved in fraud, their ethnic identity is noted; when a non-Jewish banker is involved in the same type of act, their ethnic origin is not mentioned.

Sources
  1. Hamilton & Gifford, Journal of Experimental Social Psychology (1976)

"Jews are greedy and obsessed with money"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The stereotypical association between Jews and greed is a cultural-historical construction. It stems from the limited economic roles imposed on Jews. In Christian Europe, the Church prohibited Christians from engaging in usury (interest-bearing loans). Jews, who were excluded from most professions (land ownership, membership in professional guilds, agriculture), were left with limited economic options — and commerce and moneylending were among them. They were then accused precisely for the occupation forced upon them.

Studies on philanthropic giving patterns consistently indicate that Jewish communities are among the most generous in the world. Jewish federations in the U.S. alone donate billions annually to both Jewish and non-Jewish communities. The concept of charity in Judaism (tzedakah — derived from the word "justice") is defined as a religious obligation, not a voluntary act of kindness. Maimonides (12th century) established 8 levels of charity, with the highest being helping a person become self-sufficient.

Psychological Context

The relevant psychological mechanisms are projection and displaced envy: individuals dealing with economic frustration tend to attribute greed to those perceived as successful. Literary figures such as Shylock (Shakespeare, "The Merchant of Venice") and Fagin (Dickens, "Oliver Twist") reinforced the stereotype over centuries. Antisemitic propaganda in caricatures depicted Jews with exaggerated physical features and coins — imagery that continues to spread on social media.

Sources
  1. Derek Penslar, Shylock's Children: Economics and Jewish Identity in Modern Europe (2001)
  2. Maimonides, Mishneh Torah, Laws of Gifts to the Poor

"Jews caused the 2008 financial crisis"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Historical analysis points to a recurring pattern: following every economic crisis, accusations against Jews arise. The 2008 crisis generated a wave of conspiracy theories attributing responsibility to specific Jewish financiers. A similar pattern was observed during the Great Depression (Father Coughlin's radio broadcasts), the 1998 Asian crisis, and COVID-era economic disruptions. In reality, financial crises are caused by systemic factors (deregulation, speculation, complex financial instruments) affecting entire industries, not by the actions of any ethnic group.

The findings of the Financial Crisis Inquiry Commission (2011) determined that the primary causes were: the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (1999, signed by President Clinton, with Republican support) which eliminated regulatory protections; credit rating agencies (Moody's, S&P, Fitch) identified as "key enablers"; subprime mortgages that surged from 2.5% to 15%; and complex financial instruments. The Commission examined AIG, Bear Stearns, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, Lehman Brothers — institutions with diverse management. No finding pointed to any ethnic group as responsible.

Psychological Context

The relevant psychological mechanism is proportionality bias: people tend to expect that events with widespread consequences have causes of comparable magnitude. When a global financial crisis occurs, an explanation of complex systemic factors is perceived as disproportionate. The idea that a powerful group engineered the crisis feels more proportionate. Jews, already stereotyped as a financial force, fill the role of a "big cause" within this cognitive framework.

Sources
  1. Daniel Kahneman, Thinking, Fast and Slow (2011)
  2. Financial Crisis Inquiry Commission, Final Report (2011, Stanford/GPO)

"Jews exploit non-Jews economically and view them as cattle"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The word "goy" means "nation" in Hebrew (Israel itself is called a "goy kadosh" — holy nation — in the Torah) and is used neutrally to denote non-Jews. Antisemitic distortion turned this term into a claim that Jews view non-Jews as subhuman entities to be exploited. This claim has no textual basis.

Judaism contains a comprehensive system of ethical laws requiring fair treatment of all human beings: "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Leviticus 19:18), "Justice, justice shall you pursue" (Deuteronomy 16:20), and "You shall not oppress your neighbor" (Leviticus 19:13). The legal principle "dina d'malkhuta dina" (the law of the land is the law) requires honesty in all business dealings. Jewish law explicitly prohibits "geneivat da'at" (deception) — whether the affected party is Jewish or not.

The claim that the Talmud permits exploitation of non-Jews is based on quotes extracted from their context out of 2,711 double-sided folios (5,422 pages) of legal discussion — methodologically equivalent to extracting a single sentence from a book and claiming it represents the entire work. Credentialed Talmud scholars, both Jewish and non-Jewish, consistently reject this reading.

Psychological Context

The central mechanisms are textual ignorance combined with confirmation bias: people with a preexisting negative stance toward Jews search for "evidence" in texts they cannot read (the Talmud is written in Hebrew and Aramaic). They rely on fabricated "translations" that have been circulated since the Middle Ages. The publication Talmud Unmasked by Father Justinas Pranaitis (1892) was proven to be full of errors, after Pranaitis himself admitted in court that he did not command Aramaic.

Political Conspiracy Theories

Secret control, world domination, and "the hidden hand"

"Jews secretly control the world's governments (ZOG)"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

"The Protocols of the Elders of Zion" is a text identified as a forgery, created by the Russian secret police (Okhrana) around 1903, which purported to document a Jewish meeting to plan world domination. The text was proven to be a forgery in 1921 by The Times of London: it was found to be copied almost word for word from a satire by Maurice Joly from 1864 about Napoleon III, a work that did not concern Jews at all.

Despite its complete debunking, the text continued to circulate: Henry Ford distributed it in the U.S. (The Dearborn Independent newspaper, 91 antisemitic issues), Hitler cited it in Mein Kampf, Hamas cited it in Article 32 of its founding charter (1988), and it remains a bestseller in the Arab world — in Saudi Arabia (distributed by King Faisal), Egypt (endorsed by Nasser and Sadat), Iran (first edition 1978), Japan (Masami Uno's bestsellers), and Latin America. Logically, if 15 million Jews (0.2% of the world's population) indeed controlled the world, how can one explain their inability to prevent the Holocaust, or Israel's constant need to negotiate its very existence?

Psychological Context

Research in conspiratorial thinking (van Prooijen & Douglas, 2017) shows that people who believe in one conspiracy theory tend to believe in others, even if they contradict each other. The claim "Jews control the world" functions as a "superconspiracy" — a single overarching explanation for diverse negative phenomena. According to research, this mechanism provides a perceived sense of control: if a causal agent is behind the chaos, the world feels ordered and not random, even if the explanation is wrong.

Sources
  1. Norman Cohn, Warrant for Genocide (1967)
  2. van Prooijen & Douglas, Conspiracy theories as part of history (2017)

"Jews control the media and manipulate public opinion"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The claim that Jews control the media does not withstand statistical and factual scrutiny. Major media companies are public corporations (Disney, Comcast, News Corp) with diverse ownership and leadership — boards, shareholders, and management from different countries and backgrounds. Rupert Murdoch (News Corp, Fox News) — Australian. Jeff Bezos (Washington Post) — American. Elon Musk (Twitter/X) — South African. None of them is Jewish.

Epistemologically, the claim functions as an unfalsifiable hypothesis: Jewish presence in media "proves" control; its absence is attributed to concealment. Positive coverage of Israel "confirms" control; negative coverage is interpreted as a "smokescreen." This logical structure, in which any possible evidence is interpreted as confirmation, contradicts the principle of falsifiability required of any claim with evidential value.

Psychological Context

The central psychological mechanisms are confirmation bias combined with illusory correlation. ADL Global 100 survey data indicate that this is one of the most prevalent antisemitic stereotypes — 76% of respondents in the Middle East and 24% in Europe believe that Jews control the media. When the belief exists, any identified Jewish presence in media is interpreted as confirmation, while thousands of non-Jewish media professionals are not perceived as relevant.

Sources

  2. Steven Bayme, Understanding Jewish History (1997)

"Jews have dual loyalty — they are loyal to Israel, not their country"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The "dual loyalty" accusation has served as a tool to exclude Jews from civic life for centuries. A key example is the Dreyfus Affair: Captain Alfred Dreyfus, a French-Jewish officer, was convicted of treason in 1894 on the basis of fabricated evidence, with some of the reasoning stemming from the assumption that a Jew could not be truly loyal to France. Dreyfus was acquitted in 1906.

Historical data paint a picture opposite to the claim: Jews served in their countries' armies at high rates, sometimes disproportionately. 100,000 Jews served in the German army in World War I, 12,000 were killed in battle, 18,000 received the Iron Cross, 3,000 were promoted, and 3,000 became officers. In 1935, their German citizenship was revoked. 550,000 Jews served in the U.S. military in World War II, over 50,000 received decorations, and 3 received the Medal of Honor in that war (17 total across all wars). In Britain: approximately 50,000 Jews served in WWI, over 70,000 in WWII. A Jewish Brigade of 5,000 soldiers was established in the British Army in September 1944. 30,000 Jewish volunteers from Mandatory Palestine served in the British Army, over 700 were killed. It is worth noting that the "dual loyalty" accusation is selectively directed at Jews alone, not at other diaspora groups.

Voting data provide an additional indicator: if "dual loyalty" were a real phenomenon, one would expect American Jews to consistently vote for the most pro-Israel candidate. In reality, 78% voted for Obama (2008), and only 24% for Trump, despite his pro-Israel policies. This finding indicates that voting patterns are determined by domestic American values. An ADL survey (2024) found that 51% of Americans believe Jews are more loyal to Israel, a figure reflecting the prevalence of the stereotype rather than its validity.

Psychological Context

The spread of the claim can be attributed to the mechanism of "perpetual othering." The accusation reflects a foundational assumption that Jews are always "foreign," regardless of how long they have lived in a country. This is a universal pattern documented in racism research: a minority group is constantly required to "prove" loyalty, while the loyalty of majority members is taken for granted.

"9/11 was orchestrated by Israel / the Mossad"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Within days of the September 11, 2001 attacks, conspiracy theories began spreading claiming that 4,000 Jews were absent from the Twin Towers after being warned by the Mossad. The claim was disseminated primarily through Al-Manar (Hezbollah), conspiracy websites, and Iranian propaganda.

The evidence shows: approximately 400 Jews were killed in the attacks, proportional to New York's Jewish population (approximately 12%). Cantor Fitzgerald lost 658 employees — the largest single-company loss in the attack. The source of the "4,000" figure was traced to a Jerusalem Post report (September 12, 2001), which noted that the Israeli Foreign Ministry was trying to "locate" 4,000 Israelis in the area. The word "locate" was distorted into "warned." The Syrian state newspaper Al-Thawra (September 15, 2001) appears to have first published the distorted version.

Additional data: the Wall Street Journal (October 11, 2001) reported that approximately 10% of identified victims were Jewish. An article in Jewish Week (September 2002) confirmed at least 400 Jewish victims — approximately 15% of all those killed. This percentage matches the proportion of Jews in New York's population (9% statewide, 12% in the city). 5 Israeli citizens were killed in the attack: Alona Abraham, Daniel Lewin, Hagay Shefi, Shai Levinhar, and another victim. This fact directly contradicts the claim of an "advance warning."

Psychological Context

The spread of the claim can be attributed to two psychological mechanisms: proportionality bias and blame displacement. An attack of the magnitude of 9/11 creates a cognitive need for a "proportionate" explanation — a small organization like Al-Qaeda is perceived as insufficient, and a power like Israel/Mossad is perceived as a more "fitting" cause. In the Arab world, the conspiracy additionally served to deflect responsibility from an Arab Islamist organization to an external actor.

"Jews created communism to destroy Western civilization"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Karl Marx was born to a Jewish family but was baptized in childhood, and in his writings expressed anti-Jewish positions (see "On the Jewish Question," 1843, where he wrote that "the god of the Jew is money"). It is worth noting the internal contradiction in antisemitic discourse: the same actors who accused Jews of being communists simultaneously accused them of controlling capitalism — a contradiction indicating that the motive is blaming the group rather than factual analysis.

Historical records show that in the Soviet Union, Jews were systematically persecuted under the communist regime: the Anti-Cosmopolitan Campaign (1948–1953) dismissed Jewish intellectuals, the "Doctors' Plot" (1953) accused Jewish doctors of plotting to murder Soviet leaders, and the Refusenik movement (1960s–1980s) was persecuted for Jews' desire to emigrate. Jewish cultural expressions and institutions were systematically eliminated. These findings present a picture opposite to the conspiracy claim.

Psychological Context

Monolithic thinking can be identified here — attributing a uniform political position to an entire ethnic group. In reality, Jews belonged to a wide range of political movements: communism, capitalism, anarchism, liberalism, conservatism, and Zionism. Attributing the political diversity of an entire people to a single ethnic conspiracy reflects the assumption that Jews are a uniform entity, contrary to the reality of diverse opinions and ideologies.

Cultural and Social Accusations

Claims about Jewish character, behavior, and social influence

▼ Click to expand

"Jews are tribal and refuse to assimilate"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Historical evidence reveals a fundamental contradiction: throughout most of history, Jews were prevented from assimilating through legislation and force. They were confined to ghettos (Venice, 1516 — the first ghetto; Rome until 1870), excluded from professions, denied citizenship (only in 1791 in France, 1871 in Germany), and expelled from countries.

When Jews maintained separate communities under these conditions, they were accused of voluntary separatism. When Jews did assimilate (in Weimar Germany: 25% intermarriage rate, full military service, significant contribution to Western culture), they were accused of "infiltrating" society. Logically, this is an unfalsifiable claim: preserving identity is interpreted as "tribalism," assimilation is interpreted as "infiltration," maintaining tradition is "rigidity," and change is "loss of identity."

Psychological Context

According to Social Identity Theory, majority groups define themselves partly by defining who is not part of them. Jews, as a prominent minority, served a structural role as "the Other" in Europe. Full assimilation of the minority threatens this structure, since the disappearance of "the Other" undermines part of the majority's identity. This phenomenon is documented, among other cases, in the case of German Jews who identified as fully German and were nevertheless murdered in the Holocaust.

"Jews think they are the 'Chosen People' and superior to everyone"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

In Jewish theology, the concept of "chosen" does not express superiority but rather heightened responsibility — specifically, the obligation to observe the Torah's 613 commandments. For comparison: according to Jewish law, non-Jews are obligated to observe only the 7 Noahide Laws, while Jews are obligated to observe 613. In other words, the concept of "chosenness" refers to a more stringent legal obligation, not a status of superiority.

Judaism explicitly teaches that the righteous of all nations have a share in the World to Come (Tosefta Sanhedrin 13:2). The prophet Amos wrote: "Are you not like the Ethiopians to me, O people of Israel?" (Amos 9:7) — an expression of equality among peoples. For theological comparison: Christianity holds that only believers in Jesus are saved, and Islam holds that only Muslims enter paradise. In this context, Judaism presents a less exclusivist theological position than the other monotheistic religions.

Psychological Context

The misunderstanding can be attributed to mistranslation and faulty cross-cultural reading: the term "Chosen People" was translated into European languages in the context of superiority, while in the original Hebrew it expresses responsibility. This is an example of reading a cultural text through a lens foreign to the source culture. Additionally, within supersessionist theology, the Jewish claim to chosenness was perceived as challenging the Christian claim that the Church is the "New Israel."

"Jews control Hollywood and corrupt morality"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Jewish immigrants who fled pogroms in Europe, many of them destitute, established some of the first Hollywood studios in the early 20th century. The historical context: Jews were excluded from established industries and turned to the film business, which was then considered marginal and unglamorous. As researcher Neal Gabler documents, their success in a field considered "inferior" was reframed as "control" after the film industry grew to industrial proportions.

The modern entertainment industry is a global corporate system. Disney is owned by shareholders from around the world; Netflix, Amazon, Apple TV — none of these companies is controlled by any ethnic group. Logically, the claim operates according to a conspiratorial pattern in which individual Jewish success in a particular field is interpreted as "ethnic control" — a label not applied to other ethnic groups in fields where they are prominent.

Psychological Context

A combination of social envy and the need for external attribution can be identified here. When the entertainment industry produces content that segments of the public object to, it is psychologically more convenient to attribute this to a "foreign group" than to confront the fact that the broader public consumes and funds this content. The claim "Jews corrupt morality" allows shifting responsibility from the consumer to an external actor.

"Jews are physically identifiable by appearance (hooked nose, etc.)"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The physical caricature of Jews — hooked nose, dark features, hunched posture, rubbing hands — is a product of propaganda, not a realistic description. Its historical development can be mapped: from medieval Christian art, through 19th-century antisemitic caricatures (such as the French newspaper La Libre Parole), to its peak in the Nazi newspaper Der Stürmer where Julius Streicher published weekly antisemitic caricatures.

Anthropologically, Jews are a people with broad ethnic diversity including Ashkenazi (European, light-skinned), Sephardic (Mediterranean), Mizrahi (Middle Eastern, darker-skinned), Ethiopian (Beta Israel, dark-skinned), Indian (Bene Israel), Chinese (Kaifeng community), and Yemenite communities. These communities differ from each other significantly in outward appearance. There is no uniform physical trait for a "Jewish appearance," just as there is no "Christian appearance" or "Muslim appearance."

Psychological Context

According to dehumanization research, physical caricatures constitute a central stage in the process. Making a group "look different" facilitates perceiving it as subhuman. The Nazis implemented this mechanism in the children's book "The Poisonous Mushroom" (1938), which taught children to "identify" Jews by fabricated physical characteristics. Today, the same caricatures spread on social media in the format of "memes" — the same mechanism through new distribution channels.

Category 6

Modern Antisemitic Anti-Zionism

When criticism of Israel crosses the line into hatred of Jews

▼ Click to expand

"Israel is a Nazi state / Zionism is Nazism"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Comparing Israel to Nazi Germany is defined in academic research as "Holocaust inversion" — using the genocide of Jews as a rhetorical tool against Jews. Nazi Germany established an industrial system whose stated purpose was complete racial annihilation: trains, gas chambers, crematoria, and dedicated bureaucracy — 6 million murdered. From a historical-comparative perspective, there is no structural parallel between a democratic system and an industrial extermination apparatus.

Institutional precedent: In 1975, the UN General Assembly adopted Resolution 3379 stating "Zionism is a form of racism" (72–35). In 1991, the UN revoked the resolution by a vote of 111–25 (Resolution 46/86). The revocation was an Israeli condition for participation in the Madrid Conference. The revocation of the resolution by the same body that adopted it constitutes a case study in the normative weight of the comparison.

The IHRA definition (adopted by 47 countries and 553 sub-national bodies, including Harvard in January 2025) identifies this comparison as an antisemitic expression. The rationale: the comparison weaponizes Jewish trauma as a rhetorical tool against Jews and conveys the implicit message that "the victims have become the aggressors." A distinction must be drawn between legitimate criticism of specific Israeli policies (which occurs routinely) and the use of Nazi comparisons whose purpose is undermining the legitimacy of the Jewish people.

Psychological Context

This phenomenon can be analyzed through the victim-perpetrator reversal mechanism. Psychologically, turning the victim into the aggressor allows groups that bore historical responsibility for persecuting Jews — European, Christian, and Arab — to balance the blame. The claim that "Jews are the new Nazis" provides psychological comfort but is not based on historical-comparative analysis.

"Jews don't deserve a state — Israel has no right to exist"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Denying the Jewish people's right to self-determination while recognizing that right for every other people constitutes an unequal application of the principle of self-determination. Among the 193 UN member states, many were established under similar or more complex circumstances: Pakistan was created in 1947 on a religious basis and caused the migration of 15 million people; Jordan was created by the British in 1921; Syria, Iraq, and Lebanon were created by the French and British after World War I. None of them is required to justify its very existence.

The Jewish connection to the Land of Israel is documented in archaeological records spanning over 3,000 years: inscriptions in ancient Hebrew, Temple remains, Bar Kokhba revolt coins, and ancient synagogues. Continuous Jewish presence in the region is documented even after periods of exile. Political scientist Alan Dowty found that 80% of Jewish immigrants to Palestine/Israel were refugees, a finding that challenges the colonial interpretation of Zionism and points to characteristics of a national liberation movement.

Genetic findings support this connection: a study in Nature (2010, Behar et al.) and PNAS (2000, Hammer et al.) showed that all Jewish groups — Ashkenazi, Sephardic, Mizrahi — share common Middle Eastern genetic markers. Jews were found to be genetically closer to Palestinians, Syrians, and Druze than to Europeans. An ancient DNA survey (2020) of Bronze Age Levantine sites found that Jews and Arabs share at least 50% of the genome from the region's ancient inhabitants. A DNA study from a 14th-century Jewish cemetery in Erfurt, Germany (2022, Cell) confirmed a pronounced Middle Eastern origin in medieval Ashkenazi Jews. The totality of genetic evidence points to a Levantine origin of the Jewish population.

Psychological Context

This phenomenon can be analyzed through the double standard test. When Israel is uniquely required, among 193 UN member states, to justify its very right to exist, this constitutes a standard not applied to any other country. Natan Sharansky identified the double standard as one of 3 indicators (the 3D Test) distinguishing legitimate policy criticism from antisemitism.

"The Israel lobby (AIPAC) controls American foreign policy"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Political lobbying in Washington is a common practice among many countries. According to FARA (Foreign Agents Registration Act) records, Saudi Arabia spends tens of millions of dollars annually on lobbying in the U.S. (a peak of $27 million in some years). The UAE: $20 million and above. Qatar, Japan, South Korea — tens of millions more. AIPAC is an American (not Israeli) lobbying organization operating under the same regulatory rules and with the same transparency as any other lobby.

The claim that AIPAC "controls" U.S. policy ignores the full range of factors influencing American foreign policy: geopolitical interests (Israel as a stable democratic ally in the Middle East), intelligence sharing, the defense industry ($3.8 billion annually under the 2016 agreement, most of which returns as purchases from American companies), and American public support (Gallup polls historically showed 60–70% favorability toward Israel, with some decline since 2023). Isolating the pro-Israel lobby as a uniquely powerful and illegitimate factor reflects the narrative structure of the "Jewish control of governments" conspiracy.

Psychological Context

Part of the claim's spread can be attributed to a lack of understanding of American democratic mechanisms. Lobbying is a legitimate institutional component of the U.S. political system, practiced by a wide range of interest groups. When the pro-Israel lobby is described as "manipulation" rather than institutional lobbying activity, an old conspiratorial framework is being applied in a contemporary context.

Holocaust Denial and Distortion

Denial, minimization, or distortion of the murder of 6 million Jews

▼ Click to expand

"The Holocaust didn't happen / 6 million is an exaggeration"

▼
★★★★★Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The historical evidence base indicates that the Holocaust is documented at one of the highest evidentiary levels of any historical event: Nazi administrative records (the regime documented its crimes with meticulous bureaucratic precision), testimonies of over 100,000 survivors, confessions of perpetrators at Nuremberg and other trials, liberation photographs by Allied forces, physical evidence at Auschwitz-Birkenau and other sites, demographic data indicating the disappearance of entire Jewish communities, and documents such as the Wannsee Protocol (1942) documenting the planning of the "Final Solution."

David Irving, the most prominent Holocaust denier, was debunked in a landmark court case in London in 2000 (Irving v Penguin Books Ltd). The judge ruled that Irving "deliberately distorted and manipulated history." Holocaust denial is prohibited by law in 17 countries, based on the recognition that it constitutes denial of documented facts rather than legitimate expression of opinion. The importance of this legislation stems from research showing that Holocaust denial paves the way for tolerance of violence against minorities.

Psychological Context

The phenomenon can be explained through the mechanism of cognitive dissonance reduction. For those who hold anti-Jewish positions, the Holocaust constitutes a moral barrier: it is difficult to justify hatred toward a group that was the victim of genocide. Holocaust denial removes this barrier. Research shows a consistent correlation between Holocaust denial and antisemitic attitudes, a finding that supports this interpretation.

  2. Irving v Penguin Books Ltd (2000)
  3. USHMM Holocaust Encyclopedia

"Jews invented or exaggerated the Holocaust for political and financial gain"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

Structural analysis of the claim reveals three layers of antisemitism: (1) denial or minimization of the suffering of millions of victims, (2) framing the memory of genocide as a "Jewish scheme for financial gain" (a direct continuation of the economic antisemitic stereotype), and (3) turning victims into deceivers.

The data show: Holocaust reparations from Germany were achieved after 7 years of political struggle (Luxembourg Agreements, 1952). They covered a tiny fraction of the property stolen from European Jews (homes, businesses, savings, art, gold — estimated value of hundreds of billions of dollars). Many survivors lived in poverty after the war. As of today, more than 30,000 Holocaust survivors live below the poverty line in Israel. These data contradict the claim of "profiting" from Holocaust memory.

Psychological Context

Two mechanisms can be identified: "Holocaust fatigue" and the need for moral justification. People who feel discomfort with recurring Holocaust discourse seek justification for their indifference; the idea that "Jews exploit the Holocaust" provides such justification. Additionally, in parts of the Arab world, denial or minimization of the Holocaust serves as a political tool — according to the logic that if the Holocaust did not happen, there is no justification for Israel's existence. This constitutes instrumental use of history for political purposes.

"Gas chambers didn't exist / they were only delousing chambers"

▼
★★★★☆Evidence Strength
Factual Analysis

The Leuchter Report (1988) claimed that chemical analysis of Auschwitz walls showed low concentrations of cyanide residues. This claim was refuted by the Krakow Institute of Forensic Research (1994), which found significant cyanide compounds. The difference in concentration compared to delousing chambers is explained by basic chemistry: killing humans requires a far lower concentration of Zyklon B than killing lice (300ppm versus 16,000ppm).

The totality of evidence for the existence of gas chambers includes: (1) The detailed testimony of Rudolf Höss, Auschwitz commandant, both in his confession and in the autobiography he wrote before his execution. (2) Detailed testimonies of Sonderkommando members (prisoners forced to work in the gas chambers). (3) Blueprints from Topf und Söhne (which built the crematoria), including an order for "gas-tight doors." (4) Allied aerial photographs from 1944 showing lines of people entering buildings from which smoke rises. (5) Physical remains at Auschwitz-Birkenau open to public visitation.

Psychological Context

The adoption of a pseudo-academic appearance for legitimization purposes can be identified here. Holocaust deniers present themselves as "researchers" and "revisionists" to lend their claims a veneer of scholarship. Research on Holocaust deniers consistently reveals institutional connections to neo-Nazi organizations. There is no record of a recognized, independent academic scholar who denies the Holocaust, a finding indicating that this is ideological propaganda rather than a scholarly position.

This psychological mechanism is identical to the one that operated during the Black Death (1348). The difference lies in the distribution channels (social media instead of marketplace rumors), not in the psychological mechanism itself.

The "Great Replacement" theory (Le Grand Remplacement, based on Renaud Camus's book, 2011) claims that Jews deliberately engineer mass immigration to change the demographic composition of majority-white countries. Real demographic changes (immigration, birth rates) trigger anxiety among segments of the majority population. Attributing complex changes to a single "orchestrator" provides a simpler and more reassuring explanation than confronting the variety of economic, political, and demographic factors. Structurally, this is an updated version of the "Jewish control" conspiracy, with "immigration" replacing "banking" as the alleged domain of control.

"Jews weaponize antisemitism claims to silence criticism"

The claim that Jews "weaponize" antisemitism to silence criticism contradicts the data on the ground: the ADL documented 9,354 incidents in 2024 in the U.S. — an all-time record, an 893% increase over 10 years, more than 25 events per day. Campus incidents rose 84% to 1,694 events. 58% of incidents were related to Israel. Globally: a 340% increase between 2022 and 2024 (Jewish Agency). 96% of European Jews reported encountering antisemitism in the past year (FRA, 2024). These data point to a real and measurable phenomenon, not "weaponization" of complaints.

It should be noted that there is a legitimate academic debate about the boundaries of distinction between criticism of Israel and antisemitism. Criticism of specific Israeli policies is considered legitimate. Denying Israel's right to exist alone, among all the world's nations, is classified as antisemitism. Using stereotypes about Jews as a whole is classified as antisemitism. A practical test: if replacing the word "Jew" with a word describing another minority group makes the statement obviously racist — it is an antisemitic expression.

Structurally, the claim that Jews "weaponize victimhood" constitutes a contemporary version of the historical stereotype of "Jewish cunning and manipulativeness" — the same narrative structure in a modern context.

Psychological Context

The phenomenon can be explained through the cognitive dissonance defense mechanism. When people are exposed to criticism of their attitudes toward Jews or Israel, they experience psychological discomfort. Rather than reexamining their positions, it is more comfortable to claim that the criticism itself is "Jewish manipulation" — which releases them from the need for self-examination. According to social psychology research, the tendency to avoid blame is stronger than the tendency to examine positions critically.
